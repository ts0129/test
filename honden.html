本殿

社記には「御本殿五社相殿ウチコシナガシ作四方ニ高欄有ケタ行五間五尺ハリ行五間五尺」とあり「稲荷造」と称されています。
応仁2年（1468）の兵火により、境内の殿舎堂塔の全てが焼亡し、やがて仮殿の復興があったようですが、その後に諸国へ勧進が行われようやく明応8年（1499）に再興されました。
社殿建築としては大型に属し、装飾、特に“懸魚”の金覆輪や“垂木鼻”の飾金具、それと前拝に付けられた“蟇股”等の意匠に安土桃山時代へ向かう気風がみなぎり、豪放にして優華な趣をただよわしています。

Main Shrine

The shrine is described in the shrine chronicle as an ”Inakazukuri” style building with ”Gohonden Gosha Soden Uchikoshinagashi Sakuku” (the main shrine building is five halls and five floors in the four directions).
In 1468, a military fire destroyed all of the buildings on the shrine grounds, and although there was a temporary reconstruction, the shrine was finally rebuilt in 1499 after donations were made to various countries.
It is one of the larger buildings of the shrine, and the decoration, especially the gold halo of the ”hanging fish”, the decorative metal fittings of the ”rafter nose”, and the ”toadstools” attached to the front shrine, show the spirit of the Azuchi-Momoyama period.
The style of the Azuchi-Momoyama period is evident in the ornamentation, especially in the gold ornaments of the ”kakego” and ”rafter nose,” and the ”toadstools” on the front shrine.
